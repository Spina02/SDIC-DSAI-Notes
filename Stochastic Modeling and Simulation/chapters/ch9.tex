Last time we saw continuous state sspace and continuous time, but there are processes that are discrete in time and continuous in state space.

$$
\Pr\big(x(t + dt) "=" a \ | \ x(t) = s\big) = \Omega(s,a)dt
$$

where the probability of not moving is given by:

$$
\Pr\big(x(x+dt) "=" s \ | \ x(t) = s\big) = 1 - dt \int \Omega(s,a) da
$$

$$
\Pr(x,t) = \Pr(x(t) = x)
$$

$$
\Pr(x(t+dt) = a \ | \ x(t) = s) = \Omega(s,a)dt
$$

$$
\Pr(x, t+dt) = \Pr(x,t) \left[
    1 - dt \int \Omega(x,a) da
\right] + dt \int \Pr(s, t) \Omega(s,x) ds
$$

$$
\Pr(x, t+dt) = \Pr (x,t) = -dt \Pr(x,t) \int \Omega(x,a) da + dt \int \Pr(s,t) \Omega(s,x) ds
$$

dividing by $dt$ we get:

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = \int \Pr(s,t) \Omega(s,x) ds - \Pr(x,t) \int \Omega(x,a) da
$$

This is called the \textbf{master equation} and it is also a \textit{Fokker-Plank equation}.
The first term is the probability of moving to $x$ from $s$, while the second term is the probability of moving away from $x$ to $a$.

\begin{tipsblock}[Compact Master Equation]
    We can write the master equation in a more compact form:

    $$
    \dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} (x,t) = \int \left[
        \Pr(y,t) \Omega(y,x) - \Pr(x,t) \Omega(x,y)
    \right] dy
    $$

    but this is not the preferred form by the teacher.
\end{tipsblock}

$$
\Omega(y,x) = C \text{Heaviside}(\varepsilon - |x - y|) 
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = C \int_{x - \varepsilon}^{x + \varepsilon} \Pr(s,t) - \Pr(x,t) \cdot C \cdot 2 \cdot \varepsilon = \int_{x - \varepsilon}^{x + \varepsilon} \Pr(s,x) ds - 2 \varepsilon C \Pr(x,t)
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = C \left\{
    \int_{x - \varepsilon}^{x + \varepsilon} \Pr(s,t) ds - 2 \varepsilon \Pr(x,t)
\right\} = C \left\{
    \int_{- \varepsilon}^{\varepsilon} \Pr(x + z, t) dz - 2 \varepsilon \Pr(x,t)
    \right\}
$$

where:

$$
x - \varepsilon < s < x + \varepsilon; \quad z = s - x; \quad s = z + x; \quad - \varepsilon < z < \varepsilon
$$

If we take the limit for $\varepsilon \to 0$ we can use the Taylor expansion:

$$
\Pr(x + z, t) \approxeq \Pr(x,t) + \dfrac {\partial P}{\partial s} \bigg|_{s=x} z + \dfrac{1}{2} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial s^2} \bigg|_{s=x} z^2
= 
\Pr(x,t) + \dfrac {\partial P}{\partial x} z + \dfrac{1}{2} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2} z^2
$$

$$
\int_{- \varepsilon}^{\varepsilon} \Pr(x, t) + \dfrac {\partial P}{\partial x} z + \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial z^2} \dfrac{z^2}{2} dz = 
2 \varepsilon P + 0 + \dfrac{1}{2} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2} \left|
    \dfrac{z^3}{3}
\right|
= 2 \varepsilon P + \dfrac{\varepsilon^3}{3} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2} 
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = C \left\{
    2 \varepsilon P + \dfrac{\varepsilon^3}{3} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2}
    - 2 \varepsilon P
\right\}
\quad \Rightarrow \quad
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = \left[
    \dfrac {C \varepsilon^3}2
\right] \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2}
$$

Another example:

$$
\Omega(y,x) = A \delta\big(
    |x - y| - \varepsilon
\big) 
= A \big[
    \delta(x - (y + \varepsilon)) + \delta(y - (x + \varepsilon))
\big]
$$

$$
\partial_t P = \int \Pr(s,t) A \delta\big(
    |x - s| - \varepsilon
    \big) ds - 2 A \Pr(x,t)
$$

$$
\partial_t P = A \left\{
    \underbrace{\Pr(x - \varepsilon, t)}_{I} + \underbrace{\Pr(x + \varepsilon, t)}_{II} - 2 \Pr(x,t)
    \right\}
$$

$$
\partial_t P = A \left\{
    \underbrace{\Pr(x, t) - \varepsilon \dfrac {\partial P}{\partial x} + \dfrac{\varepsilon^2}{2} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2}}_{I}
     + \underbrace{\Pr(x,t) + \varepsilon \dfrac {\partial P}{\partial x} + \dfrac{\varepsilon^2}{2} \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2} - 2 \Pr(x,t)}_{II}
\right\}
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = \left(
    \dfrac {A \varepsilon^2}2
\right) \dfrac{\partial^2 P}{\partial x^2}
\qquad \text{where} \quad
\begin{cases}
    A = O\left(\dfrac{1}{\varepsilon^2}\right) \\
    ...
\end{cases} 
$$

$$
x(t) \in \mathbb{Z}
$$

$$
\Omega(s, a) = A \delta \big( |a-s| - 1 \big)
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = \int \Pr(s,t) A \delta \big( |x - s| - 1 \big) ds - 2 A \Pr(x,t)
$$

$$
\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial t} = A \Pr (x-1,t) + A \Pr(x+1,t) - 2 A \Pr(x,t)
$$

Which is the jump from $(x - 1)$ to $x$, plus the jump backward from $(x + 1)$ to $x$, minus the jump from $x$ to $(x - 1)$ and $(x + 1)$.

So this is a Discrete Space and Continuous Time Markov Process (CTMC), becouse we have $x(0) \in \mathbb{Z}$.
